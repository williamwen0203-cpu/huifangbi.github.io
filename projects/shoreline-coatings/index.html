We developed bio-based hydrogel coatings based on calcium alginate and cellulose nanocrystals that can be applied to shoreline substrates to reduce the impact of oil spills. These coatings tune surface wettability to be strongly hydrophilic, so that oil does not readily adhere to the coated surface and instead remains on the water, where it can be more easily recovered. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Laboratory and pilot-scale tank experiments demonstrate that the coatings:

  • Decrease oil adhesion and penetration into porous shoreline materials;
  • Facilitate subsequent oil removal using low-toxicity washing agents;
  • Provide controlled nutrient release to stimulate indigenous oil-degrading bacteria and enhance biodegradation. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

This work is summarized in recent publications in Journal of Hazardous Materials and ACS ES&T Water on bio-based coatings, oil transport behavior, and biodegradation dynamics.

本项目利用钙海藻酸盐与纤维素纳米晶等生物基材料，制备可涂覆于岸线基质表面的水凝胶涂层，通过调整表面润湿性使 岸线表面保持亲水，从而减少油污黏附和渗透，使油更多停留在水面、便于回收。同时，涂层可以缓释营养盐，刺激本地 降油微生物活性，实现“预防 + 清理 + 生物降解”一体化的绿色溢油应对策略。:contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
