Through participation in multiple Multi-Partner Research Initiative (MPRI) projects funded by Natural Resources Canada and Fisheries and Oceans Canada, I have contributed to understanding how oil infiltrates, migrates, and is retained in shoreline and subsurface environments. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

Representative studies include:

  • Vertical infiltration of spilled oil in soils impacted by root netting and rainfall (Journal of Environmental Engineering); :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
  • Oil translocation pathways and processes in Canadian shoreline environments (MPRI technical reports);
  • Resuspension and transformation of stranded oil and formation of oil–particle aggregates in intertidal zones (Journal of Hazardous Materials). :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}

These insights help improve shoreline spill response strategies, including when and where to deploy coatings, washing agents, or other countermeasures.

中文简介

在加拿大多方合作的 MPRI 项目中，我系统研究了 溢油在岸线与地下介质中的垂向入渗、迁移与再悬浮过程，包括根系 网络和降雨对油垂向迁移的影响、岸线环境中油–颗粒聚集体的形成机理，以及不同岸线类型下油的转移路径与滞留特征。相关 成果以技术报告与期刊论文形式发表，为制定更精细化、因地制宜的岸线溢油响应策略提供了数据与机理支持。:contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}
